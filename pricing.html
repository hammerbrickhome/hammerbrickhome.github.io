How We Finalize Your Price

  • All prices assume typical 1–2 family homes with normal access and standard ceiling heights.
  • Final pricing depends on access, parking, material choices, existing conditions, and any hidden damage.
  • Permits, DOB filings, engineering, and inspections (when required) are reviewed and priced separately.
  • Structural issues, active leaks, mold, asbestos, and unsafe conditions are not included in base packages.

Every job receives a written, line-item proposal for your approval before work starts. Nothing is “unlimited” and scope is clearly defined so there are no surprises later.

Important Notes & Conditions

  • “From” prices are for typical sizes listed in each package (sq ft, linear ft, or room size). Larger or more complex spaces will be re-priced after a site visit.
  • Deposit: For projects over $1,000, a deposit of up to 25% is typically required in line with NYC home improvement regulations. Larger deposits may be requested only for special-order materials.
  • Materials: Standard materials are included where noted. Premium or custom selections are available at additional cost.
  • Monthly plans: Focus on maintenance-sized tasks within the scheduled visit time. Big restoration work (full sand & seal, full room repaints, large rebuilds) is always quoted as a separate one-time project.
  • Cancellation: Monthly plans can be canceled with notice as stated in your agreement. Work already completed and special-order materials are still billable.
  • Licensing & Insurance: Hammer Brick & Home LLC operates as a licensed and insured home improvement contractor. Permit requirements are reviewed before we start work that needs them.
